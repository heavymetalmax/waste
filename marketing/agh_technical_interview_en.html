Purpose of This Document

BTC Consulting is building an industrial-grade marketing and sales infrastructure for deploying BioTC systems across Poland and Ukraine. To create technically rigorous materials (Whitepapers, Engineering FAQs, ROI Calculators, and a public-facing website), we need precise scientific answers from the technology developers.

How to use: Please review each question and provide your response in writing, during a recorded interview session, or via an AI assistant. Your answers will form the scientific foundation of all client-facing documentation. Where exact figures are not yet available, please provide ranges or estimates with confidence levels.

Block 1 — Process Chemistry & Environmental Safety

Target audience for resulting content: Environmental auditors, compliance officers, eco-inspectors.

1 PFAS & Microplastic Destruction Mechanism

Given the extreme stability of C-F bonds in PFAS, how does subcritical water (200°C, 20 bar) achieve defluorination and polymer chain destruction? Which specific mechanisms (hydrolysis, radical reactions, depolymerization) are responsible? What is the measured destruction rate (%) for PFAS and common microplastics (PE, PP, PET)?

2 Heavy Metal Immobilization & Leachability

What is the fate of heavy metals (Zn, Cu, Pb, Cd) during HTC? Are they encapsulated within the hydrochar carbon matrix? What do TCLP or EN 12457 leaching tests show for the resulting hydrochar compared to untreated dried sludge?

3 Phosphorus Migration & Recovery Potential

How does HTC affect phosphorus distribution between the solid phase (hydrochar) and the liquid filtrate? Does phosphorus remain as inorganic salts in hydrochar, or does it migrate to the filtrate where it could be recovered (e.g., as struvite)?

4 Off-Gas Composition & Emission Control

What is the chemical composition of gases released during the HTC reaction (CO₂, CO, VOCs, H₂S, NH₃)? What emission control systems (biofilters, scrubbers, thermal oxidizers) are integrated into BioTC, and what are the final stack emission levels versus EU BAT (Best Available Techniques) limits?

5 Filtrate Quality & Biological Impact

The liquid phase is returned to the WWTP headworks. What are the measured COD (Chemical Oxygen Demand) and BOD (Biological Oxygen Demand) concentrations? Is there a risk of shock-loading the activated sludge in aeration tanks?

Block 2 — Feedstock Requirements & Sensitivity

Target audience: WWTP chief engineers and process technologists evaluating system compatibility.

6 Debris Sensitivity & Pre-Treatment

How sensitive is the reactor to sand, gravel, rags, hair, or plastic bags that may pass through upstream screens? Is a macerator or fine-screen filter required before HTC feed? What particle size must the input sludge be reduced to?

7 Moisture Tolerance Window

The optimal feedstock moisture is stated as ~80%. What happens if a client's dewatering press delivers 85% moisture (poorly performing) or 70% (over-dried)? How does this shift energy efficiency, reaction kinetics, and final hydrochar quality?

8 Compositional Variability & Upsets

If the incoming sludge composition changes abruptly (e.g., an illegal industrial discharge into the municipal sewer), does the reactor shut down automatically, or does it simply produce different-quality hydrochar? What is the system's resilience to feed composition spikes?

Block 3 — Energy Balance & Biogas Integration

Target audience: CFOs and financial directors evaluating operational expenditure and energy self-sufficiency.

9 Complete Mass & Energy Balance (Reference Case)

For a reference input of 1,000 kg of dewatered sludge at 80% moisture, please provide exact outputs: kg of hydrochar produced, liters of filtrate water, m³ of off-gas, kWh of electricity consumed, and kWh of thermal energy consumed. This will become the core of our ROI calculator.

10 Cold-Start Energy & Time Requirements

How long (hours) and how much external energy (kWh or m³ of gas) is required for a cold start to reach operating temperature of 200°C? How does this compare to steady-state operation where heat is recirculated?

11 HTC-D Biogas Boost Mechanism

BioTC claims a +60% increase in biogas yield when filtrate is returned to anaerobic digesters. What is the exact biochemical mechanism? Is there a risk of inhibiting methanogenic bacteria due to furfural or phenolic compounds present in the HTC filtrate? What concentrations trigger inhibition?

12 Reaction Exothermicity & Autothermal Potential

Carbonization reactions (decarboxylation, dehydration) can be exothermic. Is the heat released sufficient to sustain reactor temperature autogenously after cold start, or does the system always require external heating? What percentage of total thermal demand is covered by exothermic reaction heat?

Block 5 — Hydrochar as Solid Fuel (Combustion Properties)

Target audience: Cement plant directors, district heating operators, and industrial boiler managers (offtakers).

16 Coalification Degree (Van Krevelen Position)

How do the atomic O/C and H/C ratios of sewage sludge-derived hydrochar compare to lignite (brown coal) and sub-bituminous coal on the Van Krevelen diagram? How close does the product come to true coal-grade fuel?

17 Combustion Behavior: Slagging & Fouling Risk

Given the high ash content of sludge-derived hydrochar, what are the risks of slagging, fouling, and clinker formation in industrial boilers? Do alkali metals (K, Na) cause corrosion of heat exchange surfaces? Is co-combustion (blending with coal or biomass) required, and at what ratio?

18 Surface Area & Biochar/Sorbent Potential

How does the specific surface area (BET) and pore structure of hydrochar compare to dried sludge and activated carbon? Does HTC improve sorption capacity enough for the product to be used as a soil amendment (biochar) or water treatment sorbent, rather than solely as fuel?

Block 6 — Feedstock Variability: Three Commercial Case Studies

Each case study will be used to build a segment-specific sales pitch with verified scientific data.

We serve three distinct market segments. Each feedstock produces different hydrochar. Please comment on the expected outputs, unique challenges, and optimal end-use for each case:

Case 1: Municipal Sewage Sludge (B2G — Water Utilities)
19 Municipal Sludge → Hydrochar Performance

Feedstock profile: High inorganic content (sand, ash), microplastics, pathogens, elevated nitrogen. Typical for Polish MPWiK and Ukrainian Vodokanals processing combined municipal wastewater.
Questions: (a) What is the realistic peak calorific value (MJ/kg) achievable, given high ash? (b) Is this hydrochar suitable for direct combustion at a CHP, or must it be co-fired with coal/biomass? (c) What is the typical ash content (%) of the output product?

Case 2: Food & Beverage Industry Waste (B2B — Food Processors)
20 Food Waste → Premium Hydrochar Potential

Feedstock profile: Slaughterhouse offal, dairy plant residues, expired supermarket organics, catering waste. High moisture, rich in fats, proteins, and carbohydrates, near-zero ash content.
Questions: (a) Is it correct that hydrochar from this feedstock could achieve premium calorific values of 25–28 MJ/kg due to negligible ash? (b) How does the high fat content affect HTC reaction kinetics and product composition? (c) Are there any operational risks (foaming, clogging) associated with high-lipid feedstocks?

Case 3: Agricultural & Livestock Waste (B2B — Agribusiness)
21 Manure & Crop Residue → Soil Amendment or Fuel?

Feedstock profile: Pig/cattle manure, poultry litter, corn stover, straw. High lignocellulose content, rich in phosphorus and potassium. Often contaminated with veterinary antibiotics (tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones).
Questions: (a) For manure-derived hydrochar, is it more valuable as a slow-release soil amendment (biochar) than as fuel, given the nutrient content? (b) Does HTC at 200°C fully destroy veterinary antibiotics and antimicrobial resistance genes (ARGs)? (c) Can the liquid filtrate from manure HTC serve as a concentrated liquid fertilizer?

Block 7 — Competitive Differentiation: Why BioTC?

Critical for positioning against TerraNova Energy, C-Green, Ingelia, HTCycle, and traditional incineration.

7A — Architectural Uniqueness
22 Integrated TH+HTC vs. Standalone Systems

Most competitors offer either Thermal Hydrolysis (THP) or HTC separately. BioTC integrates both in a closed-loop system. (a) What specifically happens at the engineering level — is it a sequential two-reactor chain or a single-vessel dual-process? (b) What measurable advantage does integration provide over running TH and HTC as separate units? (c) Is the "closed-loop" claim about heat recirculation, water recirculation, or both?

23 HTC-D Digestion Booster — Originality & Priority

The concept of returning HTC filtrate to anaerobic digesters to boost biogas yield is central to the HTC-D variant. (a) Is this BioTheCon's original innovation, or is it a known approach in the literature? (b) Do you hold priority (patent or publication) on this specific configuration? (c) How does your implementation differ from similar concepts by competitors?

7B — Head-to-Head Comparison
24 BioTC vs. Incineration (Fluidized Bed)

Direct incineration in fluidized bed furnaces is the dominant method in Germany and Switzerland. (a) What is the key advantage of HTC over incineration regarding energy balance, dioxin/furan formation, and CAPEX? (b) At what scale (tons/day) does incineration become cheaper than HTC, and vice versa?

25 BioTC vs. Pyrolysis

Pyrolysis (400–700°C, oxygen-free) also produces biochar. (a) Why was subcritical water (180–220°C) chosen over pyrolysis? (b) What are the fundamental differences in end-product structure, calorific value, and contaminant destruction between hydrochar and pyrolytic biochar? (c) What is the energy cost comparison per ton of input?

26 BioTC vs. TerraNova Energy (Germany)

TerraNova is arguably the most established commercial HTC provider in Europe with multiple installations. (a) What specific technical parameters (temperature regime, pressure, cycle time, continuous vs. batch) differentiate BioTC from TerraNova? (b) Where does BioTC hold an objective advantage? (c) Where does TerraNova currently have an edge?

27 "One-Sentence" Innovation Statement

If you had to describe the core scientific breakthrough of BioTC compared to classical HTC in one sentence for a non-technical audience, what would that sentence be? (We intend to use this as the central positioning statement for all marketing.)
