### Headline:
### The Clean Water Paradox: We cleaned our rivers, but we poisoned our soil.

### Body Copy:
Every wastewater treatment plant does half the job perfectly: it returns clean water to our rivers. But where does the dirt go? 

It becomes a toxic, wet, pathogen-ridden mud known as sewage sludge. Today, we dump this mud into massive landfills, transport it at a high cost, or mix it with lime to hide the smell. 

But traditional methods cannot destroy the invisible threats: **microplastics, pharmaceuticals, and PFAS** ("forever chemicals"). They simply sit in our soil, waiting to leak back into our local drinking water. 

**The cleaner we make our water, the more toxic mud we pile on our land.** It is time to complete the loop.
