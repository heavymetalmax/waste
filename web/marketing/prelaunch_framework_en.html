Pre-Launch & Market Validation Framework

Strategic Overview

This framework adapts the initial validation phases for BioTC (integrated Hydrothermal Carbonization & Thermal Hydrolysis) to the heavy infrastructure Enterprise B2B / B2G (Business-to-Government) wastewater treatment market. In this segment, characterized by high contract values ($1M+) and 6–18 month sales cycles, validation relies on laboratory verification and structured Letters of Intent (LOI) rather than standard digital landing pages.

P0Founder, Capital & Compliance Fit

Evaluating regulatory compliance, internal readiness, and capital runway prior to starting client discussions.

1. Regulatory Compliance Matrix

• EU Directive 2024/3019: Demands full elimination of microplastics, PFAS, and pharmaceutical residues from sewage sludge, alongside strict bans on wet sludge landfilling.
• Ukrainian Legislation: Law "On Waste Management" establishes a strict hierarchy prioritizing energy recovery over landfilling; DBN V.2.5-75:2013 sets standards for sludge handling.

2. Runway & Capital Requirements

• Initial Budget: Approx. $50,000 required for lab sample testing in Poland, localizing technical Feasibility Studies, and securing legal compliance for municipal PPP/ESCO models.
• Financing Channels: Leverages EU green grants (LIFE, Horizon Europe), state ecological funds, and general contractor financing structures (INTROL Group).

Key Focus of Phase P0: Runway Validation

Because environmental project approvals, public tenders, and technical designs consume significant time, the startup runway must be at least 12 months. Founder fit requires deep engineering expertise or direct advisory partnerships with environmental chemistry departments (e.g., AGH Krakow, KPI Kiev) to establish technical authority during B2G negotiations.

Mapping the complex decision-making unit (DMU) and initiating technical discovery with municipalities and industrial waste generators.

1. Buying Center Mapping (Municipal / Industrial)

Decisions are never made by a single person. Buying roles must be mapped and targeted individually:

2. Customer Development (CustDev) Discovery Script

Conduct 10–12 open-ended interviews with target operators using the following protocol:

  • "Describe your current sewage sludge handling flow. What are the key bottlenecks from dewatering to disposal?"
  • "What is your total fully-loaded cost per ton of disposed sludge (hauling, drying, eco-taxes, and tipping fees)?"
  • "What is the remaining lifespan and capacity of your current sludge lagoons or landfill sites?"
  • "Have you faced regulatory penalties or community complaints regarding odor or runoff in the last 24 months?"
  • "What internal approval stages, engineering checks, and municipal votes would a $2M infrastructure project require?"

Market Valuation & ROI Modeling

P2TAM/SAM/SOM & Value-Based Pricing

Evaluating the true commercial volume and structuring an interactive economic payback model for target clients.

Market Sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM)

• TAM: All municipal and industrial WWTPs in Poland and Ukraine (~1,500 facilities).
• SAM: Facilities matching modular line scale (>30,000 RLM equivalent) + chemical/food factories (~350 facilities).
• SOM: High-priority sites with critical lagoon shortages or immediate compliance deadlines (~25 facilities within 3 years).

Value-Based Pricing Logic

Pricing is calculated based on the client's annual savings: $$\text{Savings} = \text{Logistics} + \text{Taxes} + \text{Fuel Value} - \text{OPEX}$$ For a 10,000 t/year WWTP, compressing sludge by 75% saves ~$112k in transport, avoids ~$30k in eco-taxes, and yields ~$160k in hydrochar fuel value, netting **$260k/year** savings.

P3Technology Brief & Integration Framework

Setting the technical integration requirements and tailoring communications across different buying personas.

1. Technical Interface Specifications

  • Feedstock parameters: System accepts dewatered sludge (75–82% moisture) directly from screw/belt presses.
  • Utility tie-ins: Requires electrical startup power, water connection, and steam/gas hookups for reactor heating.
  • Filtrate return: Liquid output (reclaiming bound water) is returned to the WWTP headworks without loading the biological stage.

2. Three-Tier Messaging Framework

Audience Core Value Proposition Key Technical / Financial Driver
C-Level & Mayors "Convert sludge costs into municipal energy independence." Operational savings, 4–6 year payback period, grant funding.
Chief Engineers "75% volume reduction without expanding drying lagoons." Fully automated 24/7 process, compact footprint, high uptime.
Eco-Inspectors "100% compliance with EU Directive 2024/3019 limits." Thermal destruction of PFAS, microplastics, and all pathogens.
