# BTC Business Model

Three-stream revenue architecture: upfront consulting, construction-phase royalties, long-term recurring fees.

## Revenue Streams

### 1. TEO Consulting (Pre-Construction)

**Technical Engineering Opinion**: Detailed 200+ page analysis of specific municipality's wastewater characteristics.

- **Fee**: EUR 20-40K per project
- **Timeline**: Weeks 1-6 (2-month engagement)
- **Recognition**: Upfront (before construction begins)
- **Work**: Sludge composition analysis, process customization, grant strategy roadmap
- **Customer benefit**: Municipality uses TEO to secure 75-90% EU grant funding

**Revenue impact**: Immediate cash flow before capital-intensive construction phase.

### 2. Construction Royalty (Project Build Phase)

**Percentage of capital expenditure**: BTC receives fixed percentage on total project CapEx.

- **Rate**: 5-8% of CapEx (EUR 250-500K per EUR 5M project)
- **Timeline**: Months 5-12 (during construction)
- **Recognition**: Upon project completion
- **Work**: INTROL handles engineering/procurement/construction; BTC provides technical oversight
- **Customer benefit**: Turnkey solution with performance guarantees

**Revenue impact**: Scales with project size; higher volume = higher total revenue.

### 3. Gate Fees (Operating Phase)

**Annual recurring revenue from operational facilities**.

- **Amount**: EUR 50-100K per plant annually
- **Contract term**: 10-15 years (long-term locked-in relationships)
- **Recognition**: Annual recurring (Year 2+)
- **Work**: Monitoring, optimization, technical support, customer relations
- **Customer benefit**: Long-term operational excellence and performance guarantees

**Revenue impact**: Highly predictable, customer-sticky (switching costs high), enables recurring valuation multiple (3-5x vs one-time sales).

## Comparative Advantage

### Traditional Model (TerraNova, Ingelia)

- **Revenue**: One-time equipment sale (EUR 5M)
- **Recognition**: 100% in Year 1 (upfront CapEx sale)
- **Customer loyalty**: LOW (can switch providers after installation)
- **Lifetime value**: EUR 5-7M per customer (one-time)
- **Economics**: Must serve large utilities (>150K population) to justify sales/support costs

### BTC Model

- **Revenue**: Spread across upfront consulting + construction + 10-year operations
- **Recognition**: EUR 1M Year 1, EUR 75-100K annually thereafter
- **Customer loyalty**: HIGH (locked into long-term contracts, switching costs high)
- **Lifetime value**: EUR 1.8M per customer (higher per-project margin, recurring revenue)
- **Economics**: Can profitably serve small utilities (15K-50K population) due to low travel costs + grant leverage

**Valuation implication**: Recurring revenue valued at 3-5x multiple; one-time sales at 1-2x. BTC revenue EUR 8M Year 3 (with 60% recurring component) = EUR 40-56M valuation vs EUR 16M for equivalent one-time sales company.

## Cash Flow Profile

### Year 1 (Market Entry)
- 10 TEO projects × EUR 30K = EUR 300K
- 2 construction projects @ EUR 300K = EUR 600K
- 1 operating plant @ EUR 75K = EUR 75K
- **Total**: EUR 975K
- **Profile**: Heavy consulting revenue, minimal royalties/Gate Fees (early stage)

### Year 2 (Scale-Up)
- 30 TEO projects = EUR 1.5M
- 10 construction projects = EUR 3M (royalties)
- 8 operating plants = EUR 600K (Gate Fees)
- **Total**: EUR 5.1M
- **Profile**: More balanced; royalties grow 5x, Gate Fees begin accumulating base

### Year 3+ (Mature)
- 50+ TEO projects = EUR 2M+
- 50+ construction projects = EUR 5M (royalties)
- 100+ operating plants = EUR 2.5M (Gate Fees)
- **Total**: EUR 9.5M+
- **Profile**: Heavily weighted to recurring (royalties + Gate Fees = 80% of revenue)

## Defensibility

**Gate Fees create switching costs**: Once a municipality has an operational HTC plant, BTC relationship becomes embedded. Switching to competitor = renegotiate 10-year operations contract = business disruption risk.

**AGH partnership creates technical credibility**: Only HTC provider with exclusive academic validation; competitors cannot replicate in 18 months.

**Local execution**: Small municipalities prefer local operators (vs German/Spanish vendors); BTC embedded as "local partner" = cultural switching cost.
