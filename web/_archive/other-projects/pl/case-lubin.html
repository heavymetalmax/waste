Sytuacja przed wdrozeniem

After Stage I modernization, WWTP Lubin (approx. 100,000 PE) was operating with a conventional anaerobic digestion system. The plant generated 36.4 Mg/day of dewatered digestate at 18% dry matter — an expensive liability requiring off-site disposal at a cost of approximately 400 PLN/Mg. Daily net sludge management loss reached 15,146 PLN/day.

Electricity generated in-house (341 kWel) covered only part of the plant's needs. The plant had no path to energy neutrality under the UWWTD 2024/3019 requirements targeting 100% renewable energy by 2045.

Zastosowane rozwiazanie

BTC Consulting developed the research and design concept for Stage II modernization based on the TH-AD-HTC technology — a combined Thermal Hydrolysis (TH) before anaerobic digestion, followed by Hydrothermal Carbonization (HTC) of the resulting digestate. This creates a closed-loop process that simultaneously maximises biogas production and converts residual sludge into marketable hydrochar.

The TH+HTC installation fits into a hall of 20×36 m, height 12 m, directly integrated with the existing WWTP infrastructure. Two deployment scenarios were designed:

  • Variant IIa — sludge from Lubin only
  • Variant IIb — Lubin + up to 20% additional dry matter from surrounding smaller WWTPs (Hub & Spoke model)

Potencjal Hub & Spoke (Wariant IIb)

Adding 20% additional dry matter from surrounding smaller municipalities increases biogas production by +85% and the annual economic improvement reaches +5,046–11,762 thousand PLN/year. WWTP Lubin becomes a regional sludge management hub, generating revenue from Gate Fees charged to satellite municipalities.

Kluczowe wnioski

The Lubin case demonstrates that for a medium-sized WWTP (~100,000 PE), the TH-AD-HTC technology transforms the sludge management operation from a financial liability into an energy-positive, economically self-sustaining asset. The combination of radical sludge volume reduction, increased biogas production, and potential Gate Fee revenue from satellite municipalities creates a multi-stream return model with a payback period of 3–4 years after EU grant.
