Principle of Operation

What nature does over millions of years, we repeat in 2 hours.

Hard coal was formed from organic matter under pressure and temperature over geological epochs. Hydrothermal carbonization (HTC) recreates this physical process in a closed device — without combustion, chemicals, or external emissions.

01

Feeding raw material

Dewatered sludge (~80% moisture) is loaded into a hermetic reactor. Odor, pathogens, and PFAS — everything remains inside the closed loop.

Feedstock moisture: 70–85%
02

Reaction under pressure

200°C and 20 bar. Organic matter molecules break down and reform. The reaction lasts 2 hours — fully autonomous, no external chemicals.

200°C · 20 bar · 2 hours · zero emissions
03

Product and water

Hydrochar comes out in the form of granules — sterile and odorless. Process water is returned to the cycle or treated for discharge. No heaps, no landfills.

20–25 MJ/kg · 100% sterility · −80% volume

Why a closed loop is key

Traditional technologies (drying, composting, incineration) have open emission points: odor, smoke, soil runoff. An HTC reactor is a completely closed environment under overpressure. Any leak is physically impossible: the pressure inside is higher than outside. This is not marketing — it is thermodynamics.

Product

Hydrochar is not waste. It is a commodity with End-of-Waste status.

HTC converts sludge from a legally hazardous waste into a certified product. After passing through the process, the hydrochar (Eco-Coal) is classified under EU End-of-Waste criteria — it can be sold, used in industry, and counted towards carbon reporting.

Technical Specification

  • Heating value 20–25 МДж/кг
  • Volume reduction vs raw sludge −75 — −80%
  • Final product moisture <15%
  • Pathogens (Salmonella, E. coli) 100% destruction
  • PFAS and microplastics 100% destruction
  • Heavy metals Immobilized in matrix
  • Product form Granules or powder
  • EU legal status End-of-Waste (commodity)

For comparison: brown coal — 8–15 MJ/kg · Wood chips — 15–17 MJ/kg · Anthracite — 30–33 MJ/kg

Applications

Solid fuel for boiler plants

Direct replacement for coal or biomass. Suitable for industrial boilers and cogeneration. Heating value in the range of lignite — but with a zero CO₂ footprint.

Additive for cement and concrete

Partial replacement of clinker. Cement plants are interested in hydrochar of guaranteed composition. Market secured by the EU directive on industry decarbonization.

Soil conditioner (biochar)

Enrichment of degraded soils: water capacity and fertility. Qualifies as carbon removal under VCS/Gold Standard methodologies.

Activated carbon (after activation)

After steam activation — adsorbent for water and air purification. Premium market. Requires an extra step but increases value 5–8 times.

Why HTC liquor in the digester is a key differentiator

Competitors implement thermal hydrolysis (TH) as a standalone stage before AD. They do not have an HTC reactor in the chain. BioTC adds HTC after AD, and the liquor (liquid fraction after HTC) returns to the digester, stimulating additional fermentation. This gives +60% instead of +30% from competitors. This is patented.
