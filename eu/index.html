Problem

Traditional sludge disposal is dying. Not metaphorically. Financially and legally.

Sludge drying eats the budget

94% of treatment plants in Poland do not have digesters. They dry sludge using purchased energy: gas or electricity. Drying requires 1,200–1,300 Wh per kilogram. Cities up to 100,000 residents pay for every kilowatt out of the budget.

Nowhere to take it

There are only 11 sludge incinerators in the entire country of Poland. Queues, dependence on a single pipe, risk of shutdown. This is not a hypothetical scenario; it is the current reality for dozens of municipalities.

EU Directive closes old exits

PFAS, microplastics, and heavy metals in traditional sludge make its agricultural use illegal. The new UWWTD was adopted in December 2024. The countdown has begun. The first deadlines for large plants come in 2033. Those who fail to prepare will pay fines.

Solution

We find a solution where everyone wins, regardless of size.

Optimality depends on waste volume. A large water utility or industrial enterprise gets its own specialized station with full autonomy, independent of third-party operators. Small communities and businesses unite in a regional Hub & Spoke cluster, sharing one infrastructure and collectively reaching a commercially attractive scale. In both cases, we find the best solution.

Scenario A

Specialized Station

For large municipal water utilities and industrial enterprises with stably high volumes of sewage sludge

An individual BioTC complex integrates directly into the technological layout of the treatment plant and ensures full waste disposal at its point of origin.

  • Full waste disposal at the point of origin
  • Zero dependence on third-party operators
  • Elimination of sludge transportation costs
  • Guaranteed fulfillment of environmental KPIs

Large Water Utilities · Food Industry · Chemical · Pulp & Paper

Scenario B

Regional Hub & Spoke Cluster

For small cities, communities, and enterprises where building a separate HTC plant is economically sub-optimal

Several adjacent participants join forces. The central Hub is equipped with a BioTC reactor, while the Spoke participants perform primary dewatering locally and transport the sludge to the hub.

  • Distribution of financial burden among participants
  • Commercially attractive volumes of hydrochar and biogas
  • Single environmental certification instead of dozens
  • Access to EU grants up to 90% CapEx
